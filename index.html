Project Overview

This website explores why hate emerges and persists in human societies. Using Social Influence Theory as a foundation, it integrates psychological, sociological, and anthropological research to explain prejudice, intergroup hostility, and collective violence.

Social Influence Theory

Read more

Social Influence Theory explains how individual beliefs and behaviors are shaped by social norms, authority, and group membership. This theory helps explain how hostile ideologies can become normalized.

  • Compliance: Conforming to avoid punishment or gain approval
  • Identification: Aligning with a group to belong
  • Internalization: Adopting beliefs as personally valid

Psychological Perspectives

Read more

Psychology explains hate through cognitive biases, emotional regulation, and social learning.

  • Social Identity Theory
  • Integrated Threat Theory
  • Dehumanization
  • Moral emotions: anger, disgust

Sociological Perspectives

Read more

Sociologists examine how power, status, and intergroup competition contribute to hate. Social structures, cultural norms, and group identity all play a role.

Anthropological Perspectives

Anthropologists study tribalism, cultural conflict, and historical patterns of violence to understand how hate manifests in societies over time.
